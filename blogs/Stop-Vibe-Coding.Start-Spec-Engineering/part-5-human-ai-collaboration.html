Human + AI — The New Collaboration Model

Part 5 of 6

The AI writes the code. The human writes the architecture. Neither does it alone.

In a world where AI can generate code from a design, what is the most valuable thing a software engineer can do? Making good architectural decisions.

The Role Shift

AI doesn't deskill engineering; it promotes the most valuable engineering work. The bottleneck moves from "can we write the code?" to "can we make the right architectural decisions?"

The Collaboration Lifecycle

  1. Product Defines What: User stories and business rules.
  2. Architect Defines How: Authors/Reviews the GASD spec.
  3. AI Generates: Implements the blueprint (Code, Tests, Docs).
  4. Formal Approval: Human reviews evidence and sanity-checks the code.

GASD as Institutional Memory

One of the most corrosive problems: architectural decisions made by engineers who've left become invisible. GASD makes institutional memory explicit by recording every DECISION in version control with rationale and attribution.

The New Measure of Senior Engineering

A senior engineer's output is measured not in lines of code but in the quality of design decisions:

  • How well does the spec eliminate ambiguity?
  • How thoroughly do DECISION blocks capture trade-offs?
  • How precisely do TYPE contracts prevent misuse?

GASD turns one-time AI conversations into permanent architectural contracts.
